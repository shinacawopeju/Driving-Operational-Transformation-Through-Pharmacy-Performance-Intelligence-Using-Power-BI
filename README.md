## Business Introduction

Specialist pharmacies operate within a highly regulated healthcare ecosystem where **timely medication delivery, accurate reimbursement, inventory control, and financial sustainability** directly affect patient outcomes and operational viability.

Unlike retail pharmacies, specialist pharmacies manage high-value medications, complex treatment conditions, insurance reimbursements, and coordinated logistics across multiple regions and courier partners.

As operations scaled, leadership required a centralized analytical solution capable of transforming fragmented operational data into actionable insights that support:

- Operational efficiency
- Financial visibility
- Delivery performance monitoring
- Medication stock optimization
- Strategic decision-making

---

## Problem Statement

The organization faced several operational and financial challenges:

- Limited visibility into delivery success and logistics performance across regions
- Increasing delayed or failed medication deliveries affecting patient care
- Difficulty tracking profitability against reimbursement and operational cost
- Lack of clarity around medication stock utilization and payment patterns
- Fragmented reporting across finance, logistics, and operations teams

Decision-makers relied on manual reports, causing delayed insights and reactive decision-making rather than proactive management.

**Business Need:**  
Develop an integrated operational performance dashboard to monitor pharmacy performance in real time and enable data-driven operational and financial decisions.

# Dashboard 1 — Delivery & Logistics Performance Analysis

## Objective
Evaluate operational effectiveness of medication delivery and identify logistics inefficiencies impacting patient service.

---

![Specialist Pharmacy Dashboard](https://drive.google.com/uc?export=view&id=1BuGHRNX4VdObY6ooi4lXdfp3zLNBByfF)

---

## Executive Summary

The dashboard highlights operational strain despite strong prescription volume:

- **600 prescriptions** served across **300 patients**
- Delivery success rate remains moderate
- Noticeable regional disparities
- Courier performance inconsistency contributing to delays

---

## Detailed Analysis

### 1. Regional Patient Distribution
- Scotland and London represent the highest patient concentration.
- Higher demand regions correlate with increased delivery pressure.
- Logistics capacity is unevenly distributed relative to patient demand.

**Business Risk:** High-demand regions experience operational bottlenecks.

---

### 2. Delivery Success by Region
- Midlands and Scotland demonstrate stronger delivery outcomes.
- South East shows noticeably lower success rates.

**Interpretation:**  
Operational processes or courier allocation may be misaligned geographically.

---

### 3. Monthly Delivery Delays
- Delivery delays fluctuate significantly throughout the year.
- Peaks suggest seasonal or capacity planning issues.

**Insight:**  
Reactive logistics planning rather than forecast-driven scheduling.

---

### 4. Condition-Based Delivery Status
Treatment areas such as Oncology and Neurology show higher delay and failure patterns.

**Operational Impact:**
- Higher clinical risk
- Reduced patient satisfaction
- Potential regulatory exposure

---

### 5. Courier Performance Comparison
- Royal Mail performs strongest overall.
- DHL and DPD show performance variability.
- Delivery reliability differs significantly between vendors.

**Root Cause Indicator:** Courier selection not aligned with regional strengths.

# Dashboard 2 — Financial & Stock Performance Analysis

## Objective
Provide financial transparency and optimize medication inventory utilization.

---

---

## Executive Summary

Financial performance shows controlled profitability but thin margins:

- **Total Cost:** £481.1K  
- **Total Profit:** £25K  
- **Profit Margin:** 5.2%

Operations remain viable but vulnerable to inefficiencies.

---

## Detailed Analysis

### 1. Cost vs Reimbursement by Medication
Most medications show close alignment between cost and reimbursement.

**Risk:** Minimal pricing buffer leaves little room for operational inefficiencies.

---

### 2. Medication Stock Distribution
Inventory spread evenly across medications.

**Observation:**
Stock allocation appears balanced but not demand-driven.

Potential overstock or underutilization risk exists without predictive planning.

---

### 3. Prescription Trends (Quarterly)
- Strong activity early in the year.
- Mid-year stabilization.
- Slight decline toward year-end.

**Implication:** Demand forecasting opportunities exist.

---

### 4. Profit by Region
London generates the highest profitability.
South East produces the lowest.

**Insight:** Revenue does not scale proportionally with operational effort across regions.

---

### 5. Loss by Region
Scotland and London experience higher operational losses despite strong volume.

**Interpretation:** High demand increases logistics and handling cost.

---

### 6. Medication Payment Status
Payment distribution shows dependency across several medications rather than diversified reimbursement sources.

**Financial Risk:** Cash flow sensitivity tied to specific treatment categories.

---

# Key Business Insights

- Operational performance varies significantly by region.
- Delivery delays are the primary driver of inefficiency.
- Profit margins are healthy but fragile.
- Courier performance directly impacts operational cost.
- Inventory strategy lacks predictive alignment with demand patterns.
- High-volume regions are not necessarily high-profit regions.

---

# Strategic Recommendations

## 1. Regional Logistics Optimization
Implement region-specific courier allocation based on historical performance.

**Expected Outcome:** Improved delivery success rate.

---

## 2. Predictive Demand Forecasting
Use historical prescription data to forecast medication demand and delivery load.

**Expected Outcome:** Reduced delays and optimized staffing.

---

## 3. Courier Performance Management Framework
Introduce KPI-based vendor evaluation:
- On-time delivery rate
- Failed delivery percentage
- Cost per delivery

---

## 4. Profitability-Based Regional Strategy
Reassess operational models in low-margin regions to reduce logistics overhead.

---

## 5. Inventory Optimization
Adopt demand-driven stock replenishment rather than equal distribution.
